Playable Area Concepts

Board-layout directions A–J. Pick the one that makes the 27 playable cells easiest and most enjoyable to read. E2–E5 riff on the Glass Cube — I and J are clickable.

Stacked Floors

A

Three transparent 3x3 floors stacked vertically. Best when the game should feel like a true cube.

Exploded Layers

B

The three depth layers are separated. Less magical, much easier to tap and understand.

Node Lattice

C

Cells become large nodes instead of translucent cubes. This removes visual clutter while keeping the 3D structure.

Focus Layer

D

One layer is large and playable, other layers sit as previews. Best candidate for mobile-first gameplay.

Glass Cube

E

One real glass cube that slowly sways. Marks are shape-coded (diamond vs ring) so they read from any angle. The most atmospheric direction.

Neon Hologram

E2

The glass cube goes Tron: wireframe edges, a scan-grid floor and neon outline marks. The strongest identity for a dark theme.

Frosted Slabs

E3

The cube sliced into three frosted-glass floors with open gaps — marks become glossy beads resting on each slab. A lighter, friendlier take.

Crystal Core

E4

A glass cube with an inner glow; marks are energy orbs, and a win fires a pulsing beam through the cube — shown here on the long diagonal.

Lattice Cage

E5

Concept C rebuilt in true 3D: a swaying cage of rods with marks as solid balls on the joints. Maximum structure, zero glass.

Gravity Pillars

F

Depth becomes height: nine pillars with three slots each. Feels like dropping tokens, so the third axis is instantly intuitive.

Step Cascade

G

Floors climb like a staircase. Every cell stays tappable while the depth order stays visible — a middle ground between A and B.

Tracer Triptych

H

Zero perspective: three flat grids, plus glowing tracers that draw cross-floor lines (here steps 1→2→3). The most readable on small phones.

Floor Carousel

I

Floors are cards in a carousel — tap a side card to bring that floor forward. A familiar mobile gesture with a strong depth feel.

Slice Scanner

J

A scanner rail slices the ghost cube and enlarges the active floor for play — click the rail stops. Context on the left, comfort on the right.
